The Art and Practice of Inquiry

Inquiry could be seen as the heart of the MBSR/CT process, and one that is most challenging for new teachers to feel at ease with. 

This two day training workshop is for people who are already teaching MBSR, MBCT or a related program (or are planning to do so) and who want to explore leading and inquiry in a way that we hope is effective, salient and empowering. We are offering this workshop  to those who have already completed the Intensive. 

We will explore "inquiry" as an ordinary conversation that we might have within our own practice, with our own meditation teachers, and that we can bring forth and facilitate in a spirit of genuine curiosity in our mindfulness groups. We will practice together and inquire into our practice, and then explore issues of timing, language, levels of engagement, group process, empathic resonance and depth. 

Some specific explorations might be: 

  • How to help people engage in the daily practice (when they are too tired, busy, or anxious to name few!)?

  • How to help people make sense of their experiences, and cultivate a sense of curiosity and openness?

  • Is there a difference between "therapy" and "mindfulness" in the inquiry process?

  • How can I quickly help someone regulate their emotional arousal so they can feel safe and motivated to practice?
