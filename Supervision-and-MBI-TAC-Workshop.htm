MBI-TAC stands for Mindfulness-Based Interventions - Teaching Assessment Criteria and is a comprehensive framework for the assessment of mindfulness-based teaching, developed by the universities of Bangor, Exeter & Oxford.  See pdf MBI-TAC. MTIA will be basing their assessment and certification of mindfulness teachers on the MBI-TAC framework. 

This workshop is designed to help you prepare for a possible future role supervising people teaching MBIs such as MBSR (Mindfulness Based Stress Reduction) and MBCT (Mindfulness Based Cognitive Therapy). During the weekend we will explore the role of supervisor, the process of supervision and also explore the MBI TAC as a model to help develop capacities and skills over time in mindfulness teachers.

You have been invited because we understand that you have spent some years in training in MBSR or MBCT, in teaching the 8 week course (at least 12 rounds of teaching),  in supervision, and also in exploring your own practice, with significant amount of silent retreat experience over the course of your MBI work. Many of you have already taken up positions of leadership in terms of supervising/teaching others in this work.  (If you have not taught 12 rounds, or have not attended many silent teacher-led meditation retreats, please be in touch with Timothea to discuss your possible involvement.)

The weekend workshop will be an opportunity to connect with other senior colleagues who are committed to this work in the region.  It will also be an opportunity to connect around the issue of certification more broadly within Australia, the region and the world, as there are moves to generate a world-wide network which may facilitate a membership function for Australian and New Zealand teachers.

(Please note that attendance will not necessarily guarantee that one is formally engaged as an MTIA supervisor immediately!)
