Training Pathway in Brief

The training pathway may be undertaken with some flexibility by students within their own time frame. The training is divided into two stages:

LEVEL 1: Mindfulness Foundations and Development

This program offers an experiential and theoretical grounding in mindfulness for those who are working in the helping professions and are interested in incorporating mindfulness in their work as coaches, teachers, therapists, psychologists, psychiatrists, counsellors, researchers and group facilitators. It will provide a solid base on which to bring secular mindfulness practice into these contexts in a way which is skillful, ethical and relevant. (It will also provide a strong base for those wishing to go on to Level II of the program.) 

 Some of the prerequisites involve:

  • Attending an eight week mindfulness course (MBSR or MBCT)
  • Attending a (5 - 7 day) silent, teacher-led mindfulness meditation retreat (providing opportunity for deeper understanding of the practice)
The Level I training consists of: 
  • Completing the Study Module (providing a strong conceptual framework)
  • Attending the 8 day Intensive (a residential training for cultivating embodiment, skills practice, feedback, personal and professional integration and building community)
Read more about the Level I training here.

LEVEL II: MBI Teaching, Supervision and Certification

This program is for those wishing to specfically teach MBSR and MBCT and involves a deeper immersion into the development of a professional teaching practice including emphasis on context, assessment, curriculum, inquiry, group process, embodiment and authenticity.

  • Attending another silent teacher-led mindfulness meditation retreat (6 - 10 days)
  • Teaching either MBSR or MBCT eight week courses
  • Supervision of teaching work (using process notes, audio and video)
  • Asssessment of teaching skills (using the Mindfulness Based Interventions Teacher Assessment of Competence (MBI-TAC)
  • Certification and registration as a qualifed MBI teacher on the MTIA website.
