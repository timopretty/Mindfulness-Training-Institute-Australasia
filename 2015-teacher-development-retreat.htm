Intention
This training is an opportunity to further develop and hone your teaching skills and knowledge, as well as developing a more secure, grounded relationship with yourself in teaching.   Through practice, teaching, feedback, reflection and investigation this training will offer clarity and learning in the following domains of teacher competencies from the MBI TAC:  Relational skills, Embodiment and Conveying course themes through interactive inquiry and didactic teaching.

Curriculum
In the basic training, there was an emphasis on leading practices and introductory inquiry skills.  This TDR will be a clarifying, refreshing and deepening experience of the work you have already done as well bringing a specific focus on:

• Embodiment and relational skills
Our capacity to hold people with diverse clinical and existential challenges depends on our groundedness in the practice of mindfulness and our relational skills as we engage and hold the group.   The teaching asks that we embody particular ways of being with ourselves and our participants:  aliveness, potency, sensitivity to self and others, empathic attunement, groundedness, equanimity, authenticity, curiosity and non-identification.  We will explore developmental movement patterns and our voices working gently to enhance the natural capacities we all have to embody and express these qualities in our teaching.  

• Clarity about course themes in the curriculum
We will examine the MBI course themes as they unfold throughout the eight weeks, getting clearer on issues of curriculum, pacing, and depth in the curriculum, including the "dharma" dimensions.

• Conveying course themes through presentations and inquiry
We will be building capacity to communicate the course themes effectively through didactic teaching and the inquiry process.  You will have the chance to prepare pieces of teaching and present it in small groups, with the opportunity to give and receive live feedback. 

Who is this training for?

This TDR is for all who have completed the MBSR/CT  Level I Intensive with MTIA, or an equivalent basic training with another organization eg. CTAD, Openground, CFM etc.  You are welcome to come and immerse yourself in this training whether or not you have begun formally teaching MBSR or MBCT.
